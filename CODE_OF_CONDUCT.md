# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment for all contributors and community members. This Code of Conduct outlines our expectations for behavior and the consequences for unacceptable conduct.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** — Valuing different perspectives and experiences
- **Being inclusive** — Welcome people of all backgrounds and identities
- **Being constructive** — Provide helpful feedback and collaborate toward solutions
- **Being honest** — Admit mistakes and correct them quickly
- **Being professional** — Keep interactions focused on the work at hand

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment** — Verbal, written, or physical harassment based on any characteristic
- **Discrimination** — Treating people unfairly based on gender, race, ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation, disability, age, or other characteristics
- **Intimidation** — Threatening language or behavior
- **Disrespect** — Dismissive or insulting comments about others' work or ideas
- **Bad faith** — Attempting to derail discussions or create conflict
- **Trolling** — Deliberately provoking or antagonizing others
- **Spam** — Repetitive, off-topic, or promotional content

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces managed by the Elyan project, including:

- GitHub repositories and issues
- Pull request discussions
- Commit comments
- Code reviews
- Email communications related to the project
- Social media posts about the project
- In-person events and meetings

## Enforcement

Community members who violate this Code of Conduct may be:

1. **Warned** — First-time minor violations result in a private warning
2. **Suspended** — Repeated or serious violations may result in temporary suspension
3. **Removed** — Egregious or persistent violations result in permanent removal from the community

Reports are handled confidentially. Decisions are made by the project maintainers in consultation with the community when appropriate.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. **Report directly** — Contact the project maintainers via email or private message
2. **Provide details** — Include specific incident descriptions and dates
3. **Include evidence** — Share links, screenshots, or quotes if relevant
4. **Stay safe** — Your safety is our priority

All reports are taken seriously and will be investigated promptly and confidentially.

**Reporting Contact:** Open an issue with the label `conduct` or contact maintainers privately.

## Appeal Process

If you believe a decision violates this Code of Conduct or was made in error:

1. You have the right to appeal the decision
2. Appeals should be submitted within 30 days of the action
3. Appeals are reviewed by a different reviewer when possible
4. The decision will be explained in writing

## Our Responsibility

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying expectations for acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate action in response to violations
- Protecting the identity of reporters
- Implementing this Code of Conduct fairly and consistently
